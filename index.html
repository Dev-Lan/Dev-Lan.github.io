I am a Computer Science Ph.D. candidate at the University of Utah in the Scientific Computing and Imaging Institute. I am currently working in the Visualization Design Lab, advised by Alexander Lex.

My research interests span various data visualization challenges. I have worked with single cell microscopy data to help researchers improve cancer treatment, and have investigated how visualizations can help review tabular datasets for falsification and fabrication.

I thoroughly enjoy teaching, and have had the good fortune of being an instructor for an Introduction to Data Structures and Algorithms course at the University of Utah.
